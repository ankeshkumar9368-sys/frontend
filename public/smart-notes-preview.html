📖 Overview

Newton's Laws of Motion are fundamental principles that describe the relationship between a body and the forces acting upon it. These laws form the basis of classical mechanics and are crucial for understanding how objects move in our everyday world.

न्यूटन के गति के नियम वे मूलभूत सिद्धांत हैं जो किसी वस्तु और बलों के बीच संबंध का वर्णन करते हैं। ये नियम शास्त्रीय यांत्रिकी का आधार बनाते हैं।

Key Concepts
5 Topics
▾
01
Inertia
जड़त्व

Inertia is the inherent property of an object by which it resists any change in its state of rest or uniform motion in a straight line. Heavier objects have greater inertia.

जड़त्व वह गुण है जिसके कारण वस्तु अपनी विरामावस्था या एकसमान गति में परिवर्तन का विरोध करती है। भारी वस्तुओं में जड़त्व अधिक होता है।

✓Greater mass = Greater inertia
✓Inertia has no units — it is a property, not a quantity
✓Example: Passengers jerk forward when bus brakes suddenly
📖 The tendency of a body to resist changes in its state of motion or rest is called Inertia.
🎯 Exam Tip: Mass is the measure of inertia. Heavier object = More inertia.
02
Momentum
संवेग

Momentum is the product of mass and velocity of a body. It is a vector quantity with direction same as velocity.

संवेग किसी वस्तु के द्रव्यमान और वेग का गुणनफल है। यह एक सदिश राशि है।

p = m × v
✓SI Unit: kg·m/s
✓Vector quantity — direction same as velocity
🎯 Exam Tip: A bullet has high momentum despite small mass due to very high velocity!
03
Newton's First Law
न्यूटन का पहला नियम

An object at rest remains at rest, and an object in motion remains in uniform motion in a straight line, unless acted upon by an unbalanced external force. Also called the Law of Inertia.

कोई वस्तु तब तक अपनी विरामावस्था या एकसमान गति में रहती है जब तक उस पर असंतुलित बाह्य बल न लगाया जाए।

✓Also called: Law of Inertia
✓Applies when Net Force = 0
✓Example: Ball rolling on frictionless surface never stops
🎯 Exam Tip: First Law DEFINES force — it tells us what a force does (changes state of motion).
04
Newton's Second Law
न्यूटन का दूसरा नियम

The rate of change of momentum is directly proportional to the applied unbalanced force and acts in the direction of that force. Mathematically: F = ma

किसी वस्तु के संवेग में परिवर्तन की दर उस पर लगे असंतुलित बल के समानुपाती होती है।

F = m × a
✓F = Force (N), m = mass (kg), a = acceleration (m/s²)
✓SI Unit of Force: Newton (N) = kg·m/s²
✓1 Newton = Force giving 1 kg an acceleration of 1 m/s²
📖 F = Δp/Δt = ma | 1 N = 1 kg × 1 m/s²
🎯 Exam Tip: F = ma is the MOST IMPORTANT formula of this chapter!
05
Newton's Third Law
न्यूटन का तीसरा नियम

To every action there is always an equal and opposite reaction. Forces always occur in pairs — action and reaction are equal in magnitude, opposite in direction, acting on DIFFERENT bodies.

प्रत्येक क्रिया के लिए सदैव एक समान और विपरीत प्रतिक्रिया होती है। बल सदैव जोड़े में आते हैं।

✓Action and Reaction act on DIFFERENT objects
✓Equal in magnitude, opposite in direction
✓Example: Rocket — gases push down, rocket goes up
✓Example: Swimming — push water back, water pushes you forward
🎯 Exam Tip: Action-Reaction CANNOT cancel — they act on DIFFERENT objects!

Memory Tricks
3 Tricks
▾
🧊
1st Law — "LAZY Law": Objects are LAZY. At rest? Stay at rest. Moving? Keep moving. Only a FORCE can disturb a lazy object!
पहला नियम — "आलसी नियम": वस्तुएं आलसी हैं। बिना बल के नहीं बदलतीं!
🚀
2nd Law — "Force Makes Acceleration" (F=ma). More force = more acceleration. More mass = less acceleration for same force.
दूसरा नियम — "बल त्वरण बनाता है": F=ma। अधिक द्रव्यमान = कम त्वरण।
🥊
3rd Law — "Punch Back Law": Every punch (action) gets a punch back (reaction) of EQUAL force! Gun recoil = rocket propulsion = swimming!
तीसरा नियम — "मुक्का वापसी": हर क्रिया की समान प्रतिक्रिया होती है!

✅ Correct! Inertia depends on mass. A stone has the most mass — hence greatest inertia.

Subjective Questions
Board Level
▾
⭐ 5 Marks
State Newton's Second Law. Derive F = ma. Give one real-life example.
Newton's Second Law: Rate of change of momentum is proportional to the applied unbalanced force and acts in its direction.

Derivation:
Initial momentum = mu | Final = mv
Change = m(v-u) | Rate = m(v-u)/t = ma
So: F proportional to ma → F = ma (k=1 in SI units)

Example: A cricket ball hit harder accelerates more — proving F = ma.
💡 Easy Way: Delta-p / Delta-t = F. Change in momentum per second equals Force!
⭐ 3 Marks
Why does a gun recoil backward when a bullet is fired? Explain with Newton's Third Law.
Gun exerts forward force on bullet (Action). By Newton's 3rd Law, bullet exerts equal backward force on gun (Reaction) → Gun RECOILS.

Also: Before firing, total momentum = 0. After firing, bullet (+p) + gun (-p) = 0. Momentum is conserved!
💡 Easy Way: Gun recoil = Action-Reaction + Conservation of Momentum (both apply here)!
⭐ 3 Marks Numerical
A 1500 kg car accelerates from rest to 20 m/s in 10 s. Find the net force acting on it.
Given: m = 1500 kg, u = 0 m/s, v = 20 m/s, t = 10 s

Step 1: Find acceleration → a = (v-u)/t = (20-0)/10 = 2 m/s²
Step 2: Apply F = ma → F = 1500 × 2 = 3000 N

Therefore, Net Force = 3000 N (in direction of motion)
💡 Easy Way: ALWAYS find 'a' first using kinematics, THEN use F = ma!

Key Takeaways
Summary
▾

Newton's Three Laws form the complete foundation of classical mechanics — describing inertia, force-acceleration relationship, and action-reaction pairs.

F = ma is the most important equation of this chapter. Every numerical problem uses it directly or indirectly.

Momentum (p = mv) connects all three laws. Rate of change of momentum = Force. Total momentum is conserved when no external force acts.

Real-life examples: Seatbelts (1st Law), Rocket engines (3rd Law), Car braking (2nd Law), Swimming (3rd Law).
